Consistently late or rushing?
This usually means your audio mode is wrong for how you’re listening, or you need to recalibrate. Each mode (wired / speaker / Bluetooth) has its own latency — if you switch headphones or speaker output, check the mode first, then recalibrate.
Audio Mode & Latency

The click is heard differently depending on your output. Wired (headphones/line-out) is near-zero latency. Speaker has added air travel time. Bluetooth adds 100–300 ms codec delay. The app stores a separate calibration value for each mode, so switching output types works correctly — but you must tell the app which mode you’re using via the speaker icon in the transport bar.

Calibration

Calibration measures the round-trip delay between the click playing and your mic hearing it, so hit timing is compared against what you actually heard, not what the app scheduled. Without calibration the Calibrate hint appears under your results. Recalibrate whenever you change headphones, speakers, or room setup.

Mic Sensitivity (Threshold)

The Thresh slider in the mic panel sets how loud a sound has to be to count as a hit. Watch the level meter while you play — set the threshold line just above the ambient noise floor but well below your hits. Phantom hits in your results (counts nowhere near the beat)? Raise the threshold. Missing soft hits? Lower it.
